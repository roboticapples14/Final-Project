Computer Science

Computer Science is a major that is rapidly growing due to the increase in programming jobs and a need for tech literate employees. Computer scientists generally enjoy challenging problems, like to learn, and are interested by technology. One thing to keep in mind before going into this field is that developers often have to invest a lot of their personal time into learning about the new trends in technology, and teaching themselves new skills in order to stay on top of the field. Almost all companies are in need of computer programmers, so a degree in CS will be very benificial when breaking into the work force.

Important Traits of a Good Computer Scientist:

  • Problem solving skills
  • Mathematical proficency
  • Willing to learn new things
  • Analyitical skills
  • Passionate about technology

    Opportunities

    A CS degree involves advanced classes in mathematics, physical sciences, and engineering. These go along the fundamental programming classes, which can start off very basic, and build to involve complex concepts of computer science, including algorithms, data structures, compilers, computer system archetecture, and artificial intellegence.
